About Me

A love for reading was instilled into me early on by my parents, and as I grew older I found that my love for reading hadn’t diminished and that it was able to help me wonder about the world in a more substantial way. I found myself asking questions that seemingly had no answers, and I was not equipped with the tools to think about the answers myself. I would not find these tools until university where I took my first philosophy class on a whim. I fell in love immediately, and knew that the best way to better myself was to pursue a competency in this newfound discipline.

I am interested in 19th and 20th century French philosophy. My primary focus has been on interpersonal relationships, ethics, existentialism, phenomenology, and responsibility. I am interested in the philosophies of Sartre, Levinas, Heidegger, Camus, Derrida, and Baudrillard. Recently I have been trying to make my way through Hegel’s Phenomenology of Spirit.

I am also currently interested in learning more about Norwegian philosophy, particularly in the aforementioned areas of interest. If you have any recommendations, my mailbox is open. Furthermore, issues of Internet privacy have been of interest to me, and I hope to explore the topic further in anticipation of my upcoming applications to PhD programs.

Here is a consolodated list of my interestes:
  • Phenomenology
  • Existentialism
  • Ethics
  • 19th & 20th Century French thought
  • Postmodernism (e.g., Baudrillard)
